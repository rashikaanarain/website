About OpenNyAI

Justice meets AI

We work to bring innovators, institutions, resources and ideas across the legal and AI domain together, to create irreversible change to how citizens experience justice in India.

A Brief Timeline

Let's start at the beginning and end at the end

2021
April

OpenNyAI mission begins

OpenNyAI ("Open AI for Justice") began as a collaborative mission between EkStep, NLSIU, ThoughtWorks and Rohini Nilekani Philanthropies, with Agami as anchor partner, to advance access to justice using AI.

2021
November

Legal NLP models launch

Three NLP models were released after extensive benchmarking: Named Entity Recognition, Rhetorical Role, and Extractive Summarizer. Trained on roughly 1,000 legal judgments, they reached about 90% average accuracy and were evaluated at SemEval 2022.

2022
Jul to Sep

OpenNyAI Make-a-thon

An 8-week online challenge bringing together engineers, technologists, lawyers, designers and business professionals to build applications that use AI to improve legal services and access to justice.

2022
September

KHOJ dataset launch

Around 40 student volunteers gathered background on High Court judges across 47 parameters, producing 25,000 data points. Released by the Chief Justice of India, DY Chandrachud, at NLU Odisha, with Civic Data Lab and the Centre for Public Policy, Law and Good Governance.

2022
December

Jugalbandi launch

On the heels of ChatGPT, the community built Jugalbandi, an open-source conversational AI stack atop LLMs, Bhashini and WhatsApp to provide critical information and services to Indian citizens. Presented by Satya Nadella in May 2024.

2023
July

1st OpenNyAI Maker Residency

Five days of intense building with 45+ entrepreneurs and innovators, 16 mentors, and cohort members prototyping solutions to problems of law and justice.

2023
August

JIVA launch

The Judges' Intelligent Virtual Assistant, an AI-powered platform to help judges and practitioners find relevant acts, laws and sections quickly, built as a prototype in collaboration with Parallel.

2024
February

AALAP launch

Aalap (Assistant for Legal and Paralegal functions in India), an instruction fine-tuned version of Mistral 7B for specific legal tasks in the Indian context.

2024
May

RAG benchmark exercise begins

With no Indian-legal-specific benchmarking datasets for Retrieval Augmented Generation, the community began building a legal dataset spanning different types of legal questions to benchmark retrievers.

2024
July

Jugalbandi Studio release

A low/no-code tool to build AI applications using natural language on the Jugalbandi Manager stack, enabling entrepreneurs and citizen developers to construct one-click deployable tools.

2024
September

2nd OpenNyAI Maker Residency

Focused on building digital public goods that trigger ecosystem-level change, with deep focus on grievance redressal, scaling paralegal capacity and resolving environmental grievances.

2025
March

3rd OpenNyAI Maker Residency

Brought together government actors (DARPG) and 30+ members of the justice ecosystem in Delhi to reimagine public grievance handling systems for the next decade.

2025
December

M.I.S.A.A.L project begins

We begin working with community members, paralegals and citizens to create an AI benchmark that truly represents quality responses where citizen legal queries are concerned. Read about MISAAL →
